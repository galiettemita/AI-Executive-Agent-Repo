# FOMO friend-testers

Target: 5-8 names. Don't ship to fewer than 3.

The bar: people you'd be embarrassed to send a low-quality alert to. That's the right bar — it forces ranker quality to be high before you ever turn this on for them.

## Bar for inclusion

- Lives in their inbox enough to feel the FOMO pain (not "checks email twice a day and is fine")
- You can text them today without it being weird (no cold outreach for v1)
- They'll tell you honestly when it sucks (not someone who'll be polite about a bad product)
- Mix of Apple + Android is fine — Sendblue auto-falls back to SMS for Android, you don't have to build anything extra

## Categories of person to pull from (use as prompts, not rules)

- Founder friends running a small team — inbox is investor updates, customer pings, candidate replies, support escalations
- Consultant / freelancer friends — inbox is the sales pipeline, payment confirmations, scope-change emails
- Indie builder friends — inbox is users replying to launch posts, GitHub notifications, contractor invoices
- Anyone who has ever told you "I'm so behind on email" in the last 90 days
- Anyone you've watched compulsively check their phone at dinner

## Anti-categories (do NOT include for v1)

- People who get <20 emails a day. They don't have the pain.
- People who already pay for Superhuman / Hey / use Inbox Zero rituals. They've solved it differently. Their feedback will be "this is fine but I don't need it."
- People who'll only give you compliments. They poison the signal.
- People who don't trust AI with their email yet. They'll churn on the OAuth screen.

## After you have 5+ names

1. Don't onboard them yet. v0.5 is week 3.
2. Send the outreach in `OUTREACH.md` to the top 3 by Thursday.
3. The non-response is data too. If 0 of 3 respond, the wedge is weaker than you think and you should pause before week 2.
